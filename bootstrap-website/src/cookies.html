1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile) when you visit a website. They help websites remember information about your visit, making it easier to visit the site again and making the site more useful to you.

Cookies are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and to provide information to website owners about how users interact with their sites.

Key Points About Cookies
  • Cookies cannot harm your device or files
  • They cannot access personal files on your computer
  • They help improve your browsing experience
  • You have control over cookie settings

2. How We Use Cookies

Digital Innovation Data Company (DIDC) uses cookies to enhance your experience on our website and to help us understand how our site is being used. We use cookies for several purposes:

Website Functionality

Essential cookies that enable basic website functions like navigation, form submissions, and security features.

Analytics & Performance

Cookies that help us understand how visitors use our site, which pages are most popular, and how to improve performance.

Personalization

Cookies that remember your preferences, settings, and previous interactions to provide a customized experience.

Marketing & Advertising

Cookies that help us deliver relevant content and advertisements based on your interests and behavior.

Third-Party Cookies Set by external services we use (analytics, advertising, etc.) Controlled by third parties

4. Cookie Categories

We categorize cookies based on their purpose and functionality:

These cookies are essential for the website to function properly. They enable basic features and security functions.

  • Security cookies: Protect against cross-site request forgery and other security threats
  • Session management: Maintain your session state across pages
  • Load balancing: Distribute traffic across our servers
  • Cookie consent: Remember your cookie preferences

These cookies cannot be disabled as they are essential for website functionality.

These cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our website by collecting anonymous information.

  • Google Analytics: Website usage statistics and visitor behavior
  • Performance monitoring: Page load times and technical performance
  • Error tracking: Identify and fix website issues
  • A/B testing: Test different versions of web pages

You can disable these cookies in your browser or cookie settings.

These cookies enable enhanced functionality and personalization features.

  • Language preferences: Remember your selected language
  • Regional settings: Display content relevant to your location
  • Form data: Remember information you've entered in forms
  • User preferences: Save your customization choices

You can disable these cookies, but some features may not work properly.

These cookies are used to deliver relevant advertisements and marketing content.

  • Ad targeting: Show relevant advertisements based on your interests
  • Retargeting: Display our ads on other websites you visit
  • Social media: Enable sharing and social media integration
  • Campaign tracking: Measure the effectiveness of marketing campaigns

You can opt out of these cookies through our cookie settings or browser preferences.

7. Other Tracking Technologies

In addition to cookies, we may use other technologies to collect information:

Web Beacons

Small graphic images used to track email opens and website visits, often used in conjunction with cookies.

Device Fingerprinting

Collection of device characteristics like screen resolution, browser version, and installed plugins for identification.

Local Storage

Browser storage mechanisms that can store larger amounts of data than traditional cookies.

Server Logs

Automatic collection of information like IP addresses, browser types, and page requests.
